# Finance Paragraph Flow

Use this guide for every finance section before polishing language.

## One paragraph, one finance message

One paragraph, one finance message. The message must be a finance claim, not a label such as
"robustness" or "data." A paragraph earns its place only if it changes what the reader believes
about a question, mechanism, identification threat, or implication.

Default paragraph spine:

1. Claim sentence: state the economic point.
2. Evidence anchor: name the table, figure, model object, institutional fact, or cited benchmark.
3. Magnitude or benchmark: explain scale, not only statistical significance.
4. Boundary: identify sample, assumption, alternative explanation, or limitation.
5. Link forward: show why the next paragraph follows.

## Reverse-Outline Audit

Run a reverse-outline audit after drafting:

| Paragraph | Message | Evidence anchor | Magnitude/benchmark | Boundary | Keep/revise |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Failures:

- two unrelated messages in one paragraph;
- a first sentence that only announces a table or section;
- significance without magnitude;
- causal verbs without an identification cue;
- literature sentences that do not state the cited paper's role;
- transitions that say "next" without explaining the logic.

## Finance Transitions

Use transitions that carry argument logic:

- from puzzle to design: "This setting isolates..."
- from estimate to magnitude: "Relative to the sample mean..."
- from result to mechanism: "The pattern is consistent with..."
- from mechanism to robustness: "A remaining concern is..."
- from limitation to implication: "The evidence therefore speaks most directly to..."
