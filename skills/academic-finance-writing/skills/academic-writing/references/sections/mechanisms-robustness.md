# Finance Mechanisms, Heterogeneity, And Robustness

## Purpose

Mechanisms and robustness should reduce a specific interpretive threat. They should not become a
catalog of extra tables.

## Mechanism Logic

Use this movement:

```text
theory -> observable implication -> test
```

For each mechanism:

1. state the theory or economic channel;
2. name the observable implication;
3. explain the test and why it discriminates between channels;
4. report magnitude and uncertainty;
5. state what alternative explanations remain.

## Robustness Logic

Build an alternative-explanation map:

| Threat | Why it matters | Test or design response | Evidence | Residual risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Common finance threats:

- anticipation and timing;
- omitted risk exposure or factor structure;
- sample selection and survivorship;
- liquidity, microstructure noise, or stale prices;
- accounting restatement or reporting changes;
- clustering, cross-sectional dependence, or serial correlation;
- multiple testing and specification search.

Do not describe every robustness table in the main text. Report only the checks that change the
reader's confidence in the central claim.
