# Finance Title And Abstract

## Writing Readiness

Do not draft the title or abstract until the Paper Framework identifies the main question, design or
model, central result, magnitude or direction, finance contribution, and evidence boundary.

If a result is unavailable, use `[AUTHOR: result/magnitude]` rather than inventing an estimate.

## Title

A finance title should identify the economic object and, when useful, the mechanism or setting.
Avoid empty claims such as "evidence from" unless the setting is itself the contribution.

Useful forms:

- question object + mechanism;
- shock/setting + economic outcome;
- model friction + market or corporate behavior;
- dataset or measurement contribution + finance object.

## Abstract

Use this movement:

```text
question -> design -> finding -> belief update
```

1. Question: state the finance puzzle or decision problem.
2. Design: name the data/model/design and sample boundary.
3. Finding: give the central estimate, comparative static, or directional result with magnitude and
   uncertainty when available.
4. Mechanism or interpretation: explain why the result moves beyond a correlation or accounting
   fact.
5. Belief update: say what changes for finance theory, firms, investors, intermediaries, markets,
   accounting, or policy.
6. Boundary: state the scope if the evidence is descriptive, local, model-dependent, or
   sample-specific.

Do not include citations, unsupported policy prescriptions, trading advice, or a list of every
robustness check.
