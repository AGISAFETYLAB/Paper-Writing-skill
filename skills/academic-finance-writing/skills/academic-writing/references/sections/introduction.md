# Finance Introduction

## Core Movement

Use the chain:

```text
puzzle -> design -> result -> contribution
```

The reader should know the paper's question, empirical or theoretical strategy, headline result, and
belief update within the first three paragraphs.

## First Three Paragraphs

1. Paragraph 1: open with the finance puzzle, market friction, corporate decision, accounting
   problem, policy tension, or model contradiction. Avoid broad economic background unless it
   creates the puzzle directly.
2. Paragraph 2: state the paper's question and why the setting, data, shock, model, or measurement
   tool can answer it.
3. Paragraph 3: report the main result with magnitude, benchmark, and the discipline-specific
   implication.

## Middle Movement

After the first three paragraphs:

- explain the design or model insight in plain language;
- name the central identifying assumption, model discipline, or descriptive boundary;
- preview the mechanism, heterogeneity, or robustness logic without listing every test;
- position against the closest literature as a debate, not as an annotated bibliography.

## Final Roadmap

The roadmap should say what each section proves. A weak roadmap says where topics appear; a useful
roadmap tells the reader how the argument advances.

Reject introductions that delay the main result, rely on generic "important topic" motivation, or
hide the contribution behind literature chronology.
