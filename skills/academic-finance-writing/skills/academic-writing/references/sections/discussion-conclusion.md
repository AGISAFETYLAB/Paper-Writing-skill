# Finance Discussion And Conclusion

## Discussion Movement

Use this movement:

```text
claim -> interpretation -> boundary
```

1. Claim: restate what the evidence supports, using different language from the abstract.
2. Interpretation: explain what changes for finance theory, firms, investors, intermediaries,
   markets, accounting, or regulation.
3. Boundary: state where the evidence is local, model-dependent, descriptive, or limited by data.
4. Implication: discuss practical or policy relevance without overstating welfare, forecasting, or
   tradability.

Use no investment advice. Do not turn empirical estimates into trading recommendations.

## Conclusion Formula

The conclusion should:

- restate the core finding and why it matters;
- identify the contribution to the closest debate;
- define external validity and future tests;
- avoid introducing new results, new citations, or new mechanisms.

For working papers, the conclusion may also preview data/code availability or appendix support if
the target venue expects it.
