# Finance Theory And Model Section

## Purpose

Theory sections should make the economic mechanism inevitable enough for the reader to care about
the math. Put the economic friction before equations.

## Section Movement

1. Friction: state the market, corporate, intermediary, accounting, or household-finance friction.
2. Environment: define agents, timing, information, assets, contracts, constraints, and payoffs.
3. Assumptions: separate essential assumptions from simplifying assumptions.
4. Equilibrium or solution concept: explain what is being solved.
5. Propositions: present each result as `proposition -> intuition -> empirical implication`.
6. Comparative statics: link model movement to observable variables, data tests, or calibration.
7. Proof placement: keep long derivations in the appendix unless they are needed to understand the
   central mechanism.

## Mixed Theory-Empirical Papers

For mixed papers, every model result used in the empirical sections needs an observable implication.
If a proposition is not used later, either cut it, move it to the appendix, or state why it is
conceptual rather than testable.

Avoid algebra-first exposition and assumptions that are introduced only to close the model.
