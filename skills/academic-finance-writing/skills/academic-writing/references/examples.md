# Finance Representative Structures

Use representative papers only for structure and argument logic, not prose copying.

| Paper type | Representative structure cue |
|---|---|
| Theory model | define friction, model environment, equilibrium, propositions, intuition |
| Empirical asset pricing | signal/factor construction, portfolio sorts, regressions, robustness |
| Corporate finance | institutional setting, identification, mechanisms, heterogeneity |
| Event study / policy | event timing, treated/control, pre-trends/placebos, mechanisms |
| Banking / fintech | market transformation, institution/product classification, pricing/risk channels |
| Behavioral finance | anomaly, behavioral mechanism, model or empirical test, implications |
| Survey / agenda | organizing framework, evidence map, unresolved questions |

## Representative Paper Cues

Use these examples only to understand organization and argument logic. Do not copy prose, and do not
treat a historical paper's layout as the current submission format for its venue.

| Paper type | Representative paper | Structural cue |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate finance theory | Jensen and Meckling, "Theory of the Firm" | define agency friction, ownership structure, and implications |
| Empirical asset pricing | Fama and French, "Common Risk Factors in the Returns on Stocks and Bonds" | factor construction, portfolio tests, cross-asset evidence |
| Mutual fund performance | Carhart, "On Persistence in Mutual Fund Performance" | sample-bias control, risk adjustment, persistence evidence |
| Behavioral finance theory | Barberis, Shleifer, and Vishny, "A Model of Investor Sentiment" | behavioral assumption, model mechanism, market implications |
| Corporate governance empirical | Gompers, Ishii, and Metrick, "Corporate Governance and Equity Prices" | index construction, valuation/return outcomes, robustness |
| Event study | Fama et al., "The Adjustment of Stock Prices to New Information" | event timing, market reaction, expected-return benchmark |
| Policy/spatial finance | Hsieh and Moretti, "Housing Constraints and Spatial Misallocation" | constraint, misallocation, counterfactual, welfare interpretation |
| FinTech/intermediation | Buchak et al., "FinTech, Regulatory Arbitrage, and the Rise of Shadow Banks" | institutional setting, platform classification, regulatory channel |
| Survey/agenda | Cochrane, "Discount Rates" | organizing framework, evidence map, open questions |
