# Generic Finance Research Paper Type

Use this profile when the finance paper type is unknown, mixed, early-stage, or not well captured by
the other profiles. It follows a common research-paper structure while remaining neutral about
domain and method.

## Source Anchors

- Journal of Financial Economics is described as covering theoretical and empirical topics in
  financial economics: https://shop.elsevier.com/subjects/journals/social-sciences-and-humanities/economics-and-finance/financial-economics
- JRFM author instructions describe original research articles with Abstract, Keywords,
  Introduction, Materials and Methods, Results, Discussion, and optional Conclusions:
  https://www.mdpi.com/journal/jrfm/instructions
- CRIBFB finance/accounting author guidelines list research, theoretical, review, and short
  communication manuscripts with section structures:
  https://www.cribfb.com/journal/index.php/ijfb/AuthorGuide

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: Research Design / Model / Setting, depending on what carries the contribution.
- Evidence core: Results / Findings / Analysis.
- Compress first: Conclusion, broad literature background, secondary discussion, and nonessential
  robustness detail.
- Core floor: protect the primary contribution section and evidence section before compressing
  literature, background, and conclusion.

| Order | Candidate section | Budget rule | Section role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Front | Abstract | venue-bound | State question, finance setting, contribution, evidence/model, and main takeaway. |
| 1 | Introduction | support/core bridge | Move from finance puzzle to gap, contribution, preview of evidence/model, and implications. |
| 2 | Literature And Institutional Background | support | Position the paper against closest literature and explain necessary market/institutional setting. |
| 3 | Research Design / Model / Setting | primary-core | Explain the empirical design, formal model, data setting, or analytical framework that carries the contribution. |
| 4 | Results / Findings / Analysis | evidence-core | Present the main estimates, model results, or analytical findings and connect them to the finance question. |
| 5 | Robustness, Mechanisms, Or Extensions | evidence/support | Report robustness, mechanisms, comparative statics, extensions, or alternative explanations that protect the claim. |
| 6 | Discussion And Implications | support | Interpret economic magnitude, market/institutional implications, limitations, and external validity. |
| 7 | Conclusion | compress-first | Restate the supported contribution without adding new claims. |
| Back | Online Appendix / Internet Appendix | venue-bound | Place extra variable definitions, proofs, robustness tables, code notes, and supplementary figures when allowed. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- If the contribution is clearly empirical, theoretical, short-letter, review, comment/replication,
  or data/method/software, prefer the specific profile.
- If the target venue mandates a different section order, follow the venue and record the deviation.
- Use this profile only provisionally when the paper type is still uncertain.
