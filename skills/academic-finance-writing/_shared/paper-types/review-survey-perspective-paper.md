# Review / Survey / Perspective Finance Paper Type

Use this profile for finance review papers, survey papers, perspective articles, literature
syntheses, state-of-the-field maps, and research-agenda papers.

## Source Anchors

- Journal of Finance: Insights and Perspectives states that Perspectives articles synthesize active
  lines of research and outline promising future research directions:
  https://afajof.org/jfip-submissions/
- Elsevier financial-economics listings describe review and survey papers in behavioral and
  experimental finance:
  https://shop.elsevier.com/journals/subjects/social-sciences-and-humanities/economics-and-finance/financial-economics/general-financial-markets
- CRIBFB finance/accounting guidelines define Review Paper as based on scientific publications and
  give a review-paper structure:
  https://www.cribfb.com/journal/index.php/ijafr/AuthorGuide
- Data Science Journal author guidance defines Reviews as critical engagement with existing
  literature:
  https://datascience.codata.org/about/submissions

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: Organizing Framework / Taxonomy and Synthesis.
- Evidence core: Comparative Summary, Open Questions, and Research Agenda.
- Compress first: generic motivation, repeated paper-by-paper summaries, long historical narrative,
  and conclusion.
- Core floor: protect the organizing lens, synthesis across literatures, and future research agenda
  before adding exhaustive citation lists.

| Order | Candidate section | Budget rule | Section role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Front | Abstract | venue-bound | State scope, organizing lens, literature body, synthesis result, and agenda. |
| 1 | Introduction | support/core bridge | Explain why the synthesis is needed, what perspective is new, and what questions the article organizes. |
| 2 | Scope And Selection Approach | primary-core | Define included literatures, search/selection logic when available, exclusions, and taxonomy construction. |
| 3 | Organizing Framework / Taxonomy | primary-core | Present the conceptual map, categories, mechanisms, markets, actors, or empirical settings that structure the review. |
| 4 | Thematic Synthesis | primary-core | Synthesize findings by theme, mechanism, market setting, theory line, or evidence type rather than by paper list. |
| 5 | Comparative Evidence And Gaps | evidence-core | Use tables or structured comparison to show what is established, contested, missing, or method-dependent. |
| 6 | Future Research Agenda | evidence-core | Identify promising questions, data opportunities, identification gaps, theoretical openings, and policy/practice needs. |
| 7 | Limitations Of The Review | support | State literature coverage, language/database limits, field drift, and intentionally excluded areas. |
| 8 | Conclusion | compress-first | Restate the organizing contribution and research agenda. |
| Back | Appendix / Supplement | venue-bound | Put search strings, long paper lists, coding tables, or extra taxonomy details when useful. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- If the target is a commissioned Perspective, preserve synthesis and agenda rather than trying to
  prove an original empirical result.
- If the review is systematic, make the scope and selection approach more explicit.
- Do not write an annotated bibliography; the section logic must compare and synthesize.
