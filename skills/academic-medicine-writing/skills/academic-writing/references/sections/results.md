# Results

## Result Hierarchy Gate

Before writing Results, confirm the hierarchy:

1. participant/sample/study flow,
2. baseline or source characteristics,
3. primary outcome,
4. secondary outcomes,
5. safety, harms, diagnostic/prediction performance, subgroup, sensitivity, or supplementary
   analyses as applicable.

Do not write Results until the primary result and denominator status are clear or explicitly marked
missing.

## Results-Only Language

Results reports observed evidence. It does not explain mechanisms, recommend treatment, argue
clinical adoption, or compare with prior work except when the article type explicitly uses integrated
Results and Discussion.

Use:

```text
Among <denominator>, <outcome> occurred in <groups/timeframe>; the estimated difference/association/
accuracy/performance was <estimate> (<uncertainty>).
```

Use "was associated with" for observational associations. Use "improved" only when the design and
analysis support intervention effect language.

## Paragraph Order

| Paragraph | Role |
|---|---|
| 1 | flow, exclusions, final analytic sample, missingness that affects interpretation |
| 2 | baseline characteristics or source-study characteristics |
| 3 | primary result with denominator, estimate, uncertainty, and analysis population |
| 4 | secondary results, ordered by prespecified priority |
| 5 | sensitivity/subgroup/safety/validation results, only when supported |

Short formats may combine roles, but the order should remain visible.

## Display Integration

Every table or figure callout must support a sentence-level claim. Do not narrate every cell. State
the headline result and point the reader to the display for detail.

For every result, preserve:

- denominator and analysis population,
- timeframe,
- effect measure or metric definition,
- uncertainty interval or variability measure,
- missingness or excluded observations when relevant,
- whether the analysis was primary, secondary, exploratory, sensitivity, or subgroup.

## Common Failures

- reporting p values without estimates or uncertainty,
- hiding denominators in tables only,
- mixing interpretation into Results,
- elevating exploratory or subgroup findings to primary status,
- reporting adverse events without denominators or severity when source material has them.
