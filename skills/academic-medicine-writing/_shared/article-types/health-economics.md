# Health Economics Profile

Use this profile for cost-effectiveness analyses, cost-utility analyses, budget-impact analyses,
trial-based economic evaluations, model-based evaluations, or economic evaluations of interventions.

## Profile Boundary

This profile owns section structure and proportional budget only. Apply
`_shared/article-types/profile-boundary.md` for hard-default and deviation rules.

## Length Budget Source

Take absolute length from the active `target_length_budget`. Protect perspective, comparators, time
horizon, cost and utility sources, uncertainty, and scenario analyses.

## Checklist / Study-Type Pairing

Pair with CHEERS 2022 and CHEERS-AI when applicable.

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: Methods.
- Evidence core: Results with base-case and uncertainty analyses.
- Compress first: generic disease burden, full parameter tables, and secondary scenarios that can
  move to supplement.
- Core floor: perspective, comparators, population, time horizon, discounting, model/trial source,
  cost/utility inputs, currency/year, uncertainty methods, and sensitivity analyses cannot be
  omitted.

## Section And Proportional-Budget Reference

| Order | Candidate section | Share of main text | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Title / structured abstract | outside main budget unless target counts it | State intervention/comparator, perspective, model or trial basis, base-case result, and uncertainty. |
| 1 | Introduction | 8-12% | Motivate the decision problem and economic evidence gap. |
| 2 | Methods | 38-46% | Define perspective, setting, population, comparators, model/trial design, time horizon, discounting, costs, utilities, assumptions, and uncertainty analyses. |
| 3 | Results | 30-38% | Present base-case costs/effects, ICER or net benefit, uncertainty, sensitivity/scenario/subgroup results, and budget impact when applicable. |
| 4 | Discussion | 14-20% | Interpret decision relevance, uncertainty, transferability, equity/implementation issues, and limitations. |
| 5 | Conclusions | 1-3% | State the economics-supported conclusion. |
| Back | CHEERS checklist / model appendix / parameter tables | outside main text unless target counts it | Carry full parameter sources, model structure, code/data statements, and extended scenarios. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- Main displays often include cost/effect table, ICER plane, tornado plot, and acceptability curve.
- Do not imply cost-effectiveness unless the threshold, perspective, uncertainty, and comparator are
  explicit.
