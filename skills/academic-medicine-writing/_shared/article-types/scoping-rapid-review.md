# Scoping / Rapid Review Profile

Use this profile for scoping reviews, evidence maps, rapid reviews, rapid evidence syntheses, and
other knowledge-synthesis formats that are not full systematic reviews/meta-analyses.

## Source Anchors

- PRISMA-ScR official page: https://www.prisma-statement.org/scoping
- PRISMA-ScR on EQUATOR: https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/prisma-scr/
- PRISMA 2020 on EQUATOR: https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/prisma/
- Cochrane Rapid Reviews publications: https://methods.cochrane.org/rapidreviews/publications

## Profile Boundary

This profile owns section structure and proportional budget only. Apply
`_shared/article-types/profile-boundary.md` for hard-default and deviation rules.

## Length Budget Source

Take absolute length from the active `target_length_budget`. Rapid review and scoping review article
types are often journal-specific; confirm the target before treating the profile as submission-ready.

## Checklist / Study-Type Pairing

Use PRISMA-ScR for scoping reviews and evidence maps. For rapid reviews, use PRISMA 2020 plus
Cochrane rapid review reporting guidance; do not claim a mature PRISMA-RR checklist unless the target
journal provides one.

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: Methods / search and selection protocol.
- Evidence core: Results / map or rapid synthesis.
- Compress first: broad background, full study-by-study narrative, and secondary categories that can
  move to supplement.
- Core floor: question, eligibility, information sources, search strategy, screening, charting or
  extraction, synthesis method, deviations from full systematic-review methods, and limitations must
  be explicit.

## Section And Proportional-Budget Reference

| Order | Candidate section | Share of main text | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Title / structured abstract | outside main budget unless target counts it | Identify scoping or rapid review format, question, sources, and synthesis boundary. |
| 1 | Introduction | 8-12% | Explain the evidence gap and why a scoping or rapid approach is appropriate. |
| 2 | Methods | 32-42% | Describe protocol, eligibility, sources, search, screening, data charting/extraction, synthesis, and any method shortcuts. |
| 3 | Results | 34-42% | Present flow, evidence map, characteristics, themes, gaps, and rapid synthesis findings. |
| 4 | Discussion | 14-20% | Interpret the mapped evidence, limitations of the approach, decision relevance, and research needs. |
| 5 | Conclusions | 1-3% | State the evidence-mapping or rapid-synthesis takeaway. |
| Back | Checklist / search strategy / supplement | outside main text unless target counts it | Carry search strings, charting forms, excluded sources, and extended tables. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- Use `systematic-review.md` for full systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
- State every rapid-review shortcut transparently; speed is not a license for vague methods.
