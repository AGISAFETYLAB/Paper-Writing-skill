# Case Report Profile

Use this profile for individual or small-series clinical case reports, adverse-event reports,
diagnostic challenges, images-in cases, or educational clinical observations.

## Profile Boundary

This profile owns section structure and proportional budget only. Apply
`_shared/article-types/profile-boundary.md` for hard-default and deviation rules.

## Length Budget Source

Take absolute length from the active `target_length_budget`, especially when the target journal uses
a mandatory Word template. Protect consent, de-identification, timeline, diagnostic assessment, and
learning points.

## Checklist / Study-Type Pairing

Pair with CARE. Target journals may also require patient consent forms and a specific case-report
template.

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: Case Presentation and Timeline / Diagnostic Assessment.
- Evidence core: Intervention / Outcome and Discussion.
- Compress first: broad disease background, speculative mechanism, and excessive literature review.
- Core floor: patient information, timeline, diagnostic reasoning, intervention, outcomes,
  follow-up, consent/de-identification status, and learning point cannot be omitted.

## Section And Proportional-Budget Reference

| Order | Candidate section | Share of main text | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Title / abstract or summary | outside main budget unless target counts it | Identify the case focus, novelty, outcome, and learning point. |
| 1 | Background / Introduction | 8-12% | Briefly explain why the case is clinically notable. |
| 2 | Case Presentation | 26-34% | Present de-identified patient information, symptoms, history, examination, and relevant context. |
| 3 | Timeline / Diagnostic Assessment | 18-24% | Show clinical timeline, workup, differential diagnosis, diagnostic challenges, and reasoning. |
| 4 | Intervention / Management / Outcome | 18-24% | Describe management, follow-up, patient outcome, adverse events, and patient perspective when available. |
| 5 | Discussion / Learning Points | 20-28% | Explain what the case adds, compare with literature cautiously, and state takeaways without overgeneralizing. |
| Back | Consent / patient perspective / CARE checklist / references | outside main text unless target counts it | Carry consent status, author statements, checklist, images, and template-required declarations. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- Do not submit or draft identifiable patient information.
- If patient consent status is unknown, mark it as a blocker instead of writing around it.
- Keep claims at the level of observation and hypothesis generation.
