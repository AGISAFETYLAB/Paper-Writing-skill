# Editorial / Commentary / Correspondence Profile

Use this profile for editorials, commentaries, viewpoints, letters to the editor, correspondence,
matters arising, comments on published articles, and invited or brief opinion formats that do not
present full original research.

## Source Anchors

- JAMA Network Open author instructions: https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamanetworkopen/pages/instructions-for-authors
- Nature Medicine content types: https://www.nature.com/nm/content
- BMC Research Notes submission guidelines: https://link.springer.com/journal/13104/submission-guidelines

## Profile Boundary

This profile owns section structure and proportional budget only. Apply
`_shared/article-types/profile-boundary.md` for hard-default and deviation rules.

## Length Budget Source

Take absolute length from the active `target_length_budget`. These article types are highly
journal-specific and often short; do not use full research-article budgets.

## Checklist / Study-Type Pairing

Usually no study checklist applies unless the piece reports original data. If original data are
present, switch to `research-letter.md` or the appropriate empirical profile.

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: thesis or response.
- Evidence core: cited support, target article evidence, or policy/clinical reasoning.
- Compress first: broad background, exhaustive citation lists, and unsupported recommendations.
- Core floor: target article or issue, central claim, support, counterpoint/limitation, and concise
  implication must be explicit.

## Section And Proportional-Budget Reference

| Order | Candidate section | Share of main text | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Title / short abstract only if required | outside main budget unless target counts it | Identify the issue, article, or viewpoint. |
| 1 | Opening Thesis | 18-25% | State the central argument or response immediately. |
| 2 | Evidence / Reasoning | 35-50% | Support the argument with cited evidence, target-article detail, policy logic, or clinical reasoning. |
| 3 | Counterpoint / Boundary | 15-22% | Acknowledge limits, alternative interpretation, uncertainty, or reply constraints. |
| 4 | Implication / Closing | 12-18% | Give the concise clinical, policy, methodological, or editorial implication. |
| Back | References / disclosures | outside main text unless target counts it | Keep references and disclosures within the target article-type cap. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- Do not smuggle unreviewed original results into commentary.
- For a short report of original data, use `research-letter.md`; for a field synthesis, use
  `review-perspective.md`.
