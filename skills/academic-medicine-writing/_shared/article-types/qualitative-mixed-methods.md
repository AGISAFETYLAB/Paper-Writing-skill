# Qualitative / Mixed Methods Profile

Use this profile for qualitative studies, interview/focus-group studies, ethnography, thematic
analysis, implementation qualitative research, and mixed-methods health studies that integrate
qualitative and quantitative evidence.

## Source Anchors

- COREQ on EQUATOR: https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/coreq/
- SRQR on EQUATOR: https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines/srqr/
- Mixed-methods study-design index on EQUATOR: https://www.equator-network.org/reporting-guidelines-study-design/mixed-methods-studies/

## Profile Boundary

This profile owns section structure and proportional budget only. Apply
`_shared/article-types/profile-boundary.md` for hard-default and deviation rules.

## Length Budget Source

Take absolute length from the active `target_length_budget`. Qualitative reports may require more
Methods and Findings space for sampling, reflexivity, analytic approach, and participant quotations.

## Checklist / Study-Type Pairing

Use SRQR for broad qualitative studies and COREQ for interviews/focus groups. For mixed methods, add
the quantitative design checklist and explicitly plan integration/joint display evidence.

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: Methods / qualitative or mixed-methods approach.
- Evidence core: Findings and integration.
- Compress first: generic background, excessive quote blocks, and quantitative detail not needed for
  integration.
- Core floor: research paradigm, sampling, setting, data collection, researcher reflexivity,
  analysis, trustworthiness, participant characteristics, themes, and integration logic cannot be
  omitted.

## Section And Proportional-Budget Reference

| Order | Candidate section | Share of main text | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Title / abstract | outside main budget unless target counts it | Identify qualitative or mixed-methods design, setting, participants, and main theme/integration finding. |
| 1 | Introduction | 10-14% | Motivate the phenomenon, practice gap, and research question. |
| 2 | Methods | 30-40% | Describe design, paradigm, setting, sampling, recruitment, data collection, reflexivity, analysis, trustworthiness, and ethics. |
| 3 | Findings / Results | 32-42% | Present themes, supporting evidence, participant context, deviant cases, quantitative findings if integrated, and joint displays. |
| 4 | Discussion | 14-20% | Interpret themes/integration, implications, transferability, limitations, and future work. |
| 5 | Conclusions | 1-3% | State the supported qualitative or mixed-methods takeaway. |
| Back | COREQ/SRQR checklist / instruments / coding supplement | outside main text unless target counts it | Carry interview guides, codebook excerpts, joint displays, and reflexivity details. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- Do not present participant quotations as numerical prevalence unless the analytic plan supports it.
- For mixed methods, every integration claim must name where qualitative and quantitative strands
  converge, diverge, or expand one another.
