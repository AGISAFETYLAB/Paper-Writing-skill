# Review / Perspective Profile

Use this profile for narrative reviews, perspectives, comments, viewpoints, policy-facing essays, or
state-of-the-field synthesis that is not a systematic review or meta-analysis.

## Profile Boundary

This profile owns section structure and proportional budget only. Apply
`_shared/article-types/profile-boundary.md` for hard-default and deviation rules.

## Length Budget Source

Take absolute length from the active `target_length_budget`. For target journals such as Nature
Medicine, distinguish Review, Perspective, Comment, and Correspondence before budgeting.

## Checklist / Study-Type Pairing

Do not claim PRISMA compliance unless systematic methods were used. Use citation and claim
verification discipline even when no reporting checklist applies.

## Priority Contract

- Primary core: Synthesis / argument sections.
- Evidence core: cited evidence and any framework/table that supports the thesis.
- Compress first: broad historical background, redundant study descriptions, and speculative future
  directions.
- Core floor: the central thesis, evidence synthesis, limitations/uncertainties, and implications
  must be explicit.

## Section And Proportional-Budget Reference

| Order | Candidate section | Share of main text | Section role |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Front | Title / abstract or standfirst if required | outside main budget unless target counts it | State the review or perspective thesis and scope. |
| 1 | Introduction / Scope | 10-14% | Define the problem, audience, scope, and central argument. |
| 2 | Evidence Landscape | 24-32% | Synthesize major evidence streams, not a study-by-study list. |
| 3 | Framework / Thematic Analysis | 28-36% | Organize mechanisms, taxonomy, policy options, clinical implications, or research agenda. |
| 4 | Limitations / Controversies | 12-18% | State uncertainty, disagreements, evidence gaps, and boundary conditions. |
| 5 | Implications / Conclusions | 12-18% | Translate the synthesis into cautious clinical, research, or policy implications. |
| Back | References / boxes / figures | outside main text unless target counts it | Carry schematic figures, summary tables, and selective references. |

## Flexible Adjustment Notes

- Use a systematic-review profile if the manuscript reports a formal search, screening, extraction,
  and synthesis protocol.
- Keep claims citation-backed; narrative format does not permit unsupported generalization.
